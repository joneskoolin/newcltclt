Welcome!

Everything in this city is worth waiting in line for.

Charlotte is one of the 25 largest cities in the United States and also the largest city in North Carolina. It is located in south/central North Carolina. It is home to over 10 Fortune 1000 companies, including household names such as Bank of America, Lowe's, and Wachovia Corp.

Charlotte has a humid sub-tropical climate that is characterized by mild winters and hot, humid summers. The coldest month of the year is January with an average daily temperature of 51 degrees Fahrenheit. July is the hottest month of the year with an average daily temperature of 90 degrees Fahrenheit.

Sports Around the City

There is no lack of spectator sports in Charlotte. Long known as the home of the Charlotte Motor Speedway (CMS), the Mecca of NASCAR, 2010 saw the opening of the NASCAR Hall of Fame in downtown Charlotte, offering yet another reason for rabid NASCAR fans to visit. CMS is the largest facility in the Southeast and recognized as one of the finest in the country. With the Speedway's capacity of nearly 180,000, the attendance for its largest race, The Coca-Cola 600, has the second-largest attendance of all sporting events in the nation. CMS is also the site for race team testing and automobile manufacturing research.

NFL owners unanimously selected North Carolina as the 29th NFL franchise in 1993. Just ten years later, the Carolina Panthers won a trip to play in Super Bowl XXXVIII against the New England Patriots. The 2010 average attendance for the Panthers was 72,597, one of the highest in the NFL for that season, and a tribute to their continuing success. The Panther's home is the $187 million state-of-the-art Bank of America Stadium in downtown Charlotte.

When the Charlotte Hornets relocated from Charlotte to New Orleans, the NBA promised Charlotte a new team and they got one in 2004 - The Carolina Bobcats. In 2010, basketball legend Michael Jordan announced he was buying the majority ownership of the team. The $260 million Time Warner Cable Arena was built in city center specifically for the NBA team but the arena is also used by the Charlotte Checkers of the National Hockey League as well as for other major events.

In addition to these major professional sports teams, there is also professional baseball, lacrosse, golf, and soccer as well as collegiate football and basketball events in the area..

How to get here

Plane: By Air: Charlotte-Douglas International Airport is the 7th largest airport in the world based on traffic movement as of 2010. It has 91 gates and serves most national and regional airlines. It is USAirways largest domestic hub. Ground transportation to Charlotte includes taxis, shuttles, car rentals and CATS' (Charlotte Area Transport System) Sprinter Enhanced Bus Service, which connects the airport to the downtown Charlotte Transportation Center..

Train: By Train: Charlotte is served daily by three Amtrak routes. The Crescent connects New York, Philadelphia, Baltimore, Washington, D.C., Charlottesville, and Greensboro to the north, and Greenville, Atlanta, Birmingham and New Orleans with Charlotte. The Carolinian connects to New York, Philadelphia, Baltimore, Washington, D.C., Richmond, Raleigh, Durham, and Greensboro. The Piedmont connects to Raleigh, Durham and Greensboro.

Car: By Car: Two major interstate highways, I-85 and I-77, intersect near the center of Charlotte. Charlotte's partially completed beltway, I-485, will have a total circumference of approximately 67 miles and has been slated for completion by 2014. The I-277 loop freeway referred to as the John Belk Freeway and the Brookshire Freeway encircles uptown Charlotte, while Charlotte Route 4 links major roads in a loop between I-277 and I-485.
